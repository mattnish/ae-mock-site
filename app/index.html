AdverseEvents Explorer Features

The new market standard for interpreting complex side effect information

RxCost

RxCost® is the first standardized methodology to determine direct costs of adverse drug events, enabling decisions to be made based on total cost of care.

RxSignal

RxSignal® is a predictive algorithm that alerts users to emerging and/or previously unidentified side effect threats that may prompt a future FDA regulatory action.

RxScore

Similar to a FICO credit score, RxScore® is the first drug safety scoring system that quickly summarizes comprehensive post-approval drug safety issues.

RxOutcome

Gain deep insight into drug indications, mechanisms of action, and drug classes and their associated drugs.

Reporting

Proprietary special reports, updates on new drug approvals, FDA trigger events, journals and research publicats, and more.

Portfolio

Create and track your own portfolio of companies and drugs to analyze data across your specific interests.

The Pharmacy Director was able to effectively implement a Comparative Safety Research (CSR) framework through the licensing of AdverseEvents Explorer Drug Safety Monitor, which provides insight on all newly approved drugs using the new drug’s clinical trial and label ADE information and comparing it to the post-approval ADE data of drugs on the market. After the new drug was on the market for 2-4 months, AdverseEvents submitted Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests to provide the most up-to-date data possible for the new drug, ensuring that the P&T committee had the best insight into the drug’s CSR profile.

Pharmacy Director
Large Blue Plan Pharmacy

Access to accurate real-world ADE information via the web platform, AdverseEvents Explorer, enabled clinical pharmacists within the system to quickly use commonly accepted statistical measures like reporting odds ratios. This enabled them to rapidly determine the relative frequency of an ADE occurring. They were able to immediately determine if an event that occurred in-facility was part of a trend or an isolated incident. Once identified as a trend, the Pharmacy Director was able to use relative frequency class comparator tools in AdverseEvents Explorer to identify alternative drugs to mitigate the risk of future events.

Corporate Health System

Seeing a report that AdverseEvents had recently released, the analyst subscribed to AdverseEvents Explorer. The analyst identified 20+ drugs that had an active RxSignal®, indicating an 80% likelihood of experiencing an FDA action. He was further able to identify a drug with an associated RxSignal® manufactured by a company that had been trading close to its 52-week high. With this data, he was able to recommend that his Portfolio Manager put on a short position. Sixty days later the FDA announced a black box warning for the drug’s label and the stock plummeted.
